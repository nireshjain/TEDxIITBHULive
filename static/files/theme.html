IITBHULive : The Next Chapter

George Orwell once wrote a novel called 1984. He sat in a small room, fought his soon-to-be-fatal TB and penned down what is probably the most popular dystopian novel ever. He spoke of a totalitarian and dictatorial world where free thinking was a crime, emotions were banned, dogma was the norm and two plus two equalled five. In short, George Orwell presented a very grim future for mankind.

Cue to real life, and in 1984, the first Macs go on sale, Britain grants Brunei independence, Rakesh Sharma becomes the first Indian to go into space, and Scarlett Johansson is born. All in all, not a bad year 1984 turned out be! But, wait! 1984 also saw one more very important occasion - the very first TED conference.

TED was a haven for the curious and the adventurous, the open-minded intelligentsia. And ever since their conferences have astounded the world with their unfailing verve for the new, for the amazing and for the beautiful. Each year they have strengthened independent thought and the spirit of inquiry.

And now TED celebrates its 30th birthday. And for its 30th birthday, TED will contemplate the future, just as Orwell once did, albeit with a little more context and a lot more chutzpah!

Welcome to "The Next Chapter" where great minds and leading experts will come together and look back at the past thirty years, scrutinize all of the successes, analyze the failures. They will then, stand on the shoulders of their past and contemporary giants, and they will contemplate the future - the future of technology, entertainment and design. Will we have the robot servants that Asimov wrote of? Or only melting glaciers? Will Google Glass help us appreciate the beauty around us more? Or will it only entomb us further in our virtual worlds?

Nothing will be off-limits, no stone will be left unturned!

Join us as the luminaries of the world discuss The Next Chapter of mankind.
